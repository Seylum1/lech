01 Your account & the Citizen Portal

Everything you do on the Imperial Network runs through a single account. Open the Citizen Portal from the button in the top-right corner of any page.

  • New here? Choose Register, enter your Minecraft username (we verify it is real), pick a password, and create your account.
  • Already have an account? Choose Sign In and use the same username and password across every Ministry.
  • Once signed in, the portal shows your standing, identity card, honours, citizenship, and account settings all in one place.
Your username is your Minecraft username — there is nothing extra to remember.

02 From Registered Alien to citizen

Every brand-new account begins as a Registered Alien. That means you are known to the Empire but not yet a citizen.

Citizenship is granted by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA), not automatically. If you ticked "I hold citizenship" when registering, MOFA has already been asked to review you. Otherwise, visit the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and ask for your status to be reviewed.

→ Ministry of Foreign Affairs

03 Your identity card & profile picture

Once the Ministry of the Interior assigns you a province of residency, a provincial identity card is issued to you automatically and appears in your portal.

You can set a profile picture from the Manage Account panel in the portal — it appears on your identity card. If you don't upload one, your Minecraft face is used by default.

If the Ministry of the Interior has locked your picture, the upload controls are hidden and only they can change it.

04 Changing your password

In the portal's Manage Account panel, enter your current password and a new one to update it. That's all it takes.

If you've forgotten your password entirely, contact the Ministry of the Interior, who can reset it for you.

05 Visas & travel

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs handles visas, travel advisories, and citizenship records.

  • Apply for a visa on the MOFA page and note your reference number.
  • Use Track Application with that reference to follow its progress.
  • Check travel advisories before visiting other states.

→ Ministry of Foreign Affairs

06 Reading the law

The Empire's laws and its courts are open to read — you do not need any special access.

→ The Bundeskongress — bills, sessions, and the members of both chambers.

→ Imperial Judiciary — statutes, cases, and judgments.

07 Other citizen services

Most Ministries offer something useful to an ordinary resident:

→ Ministry of Finance — register a business or view the business registry.

→ Maritime Commission — register a vessel or request a berth.

→ Imperial Household — honours, awards, and the peerage register.

Anything you own or hold — businesses, vessels, honours, seats — is gathered for you automatically in the Citizen Portal.

08 Getting help

Account problems — a forgotten password, a wrong name, access questions — are handled by the Ministry of the Interior. Citizenship and travel questions go to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

When in doubt, open the Citizen Portal: your standing and records are all there, and each panel links to the Ministry that manages it.
